ASERTH is the 'QWERTY-flip-twist'

The re-arrangement scheme is **much easier to remember** 
than Colemak for people already proficient with QWERTY,
and thus it is **faster to learn** while being **almost**
as efficient as Colemak:

 * flip keys: 
	- ERT with DFG
    - N with J
    - K with I
 * twist or rotate (counter-clockwise): L → ; → P → O → L

## Combining diacritics:
 - ⌥w  : ◌̀ (U+0300) combining grave accent    (&#x0300;)
 - ⌥e  : ◌́ (U+0301) combining acute accent
 - ⌥i  : ◌̈ (U+0308) combining diaeresis/umlaut (eg: naïve)
 - ⌥⇧n : ◌̃ (U+0303) combining tilde /ˈtɪldə/˜
